Some Facts About China

China is the largest Asian country with a total area of over 9 million square kilometers. All the people living in this country makes up one-fifth of all humans in the world, with a population of more than 1 billion people. China is one of the few existing countries in the world that have flourished economically and culturally in its early stages. Little to no outside influences had a large impact on China’s cultural development (with the expansion of Buddhism coming from India).

Popular Cities in China


Places to Visit in Beijing


Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of connected fortifications and served as protection of Imperial China from nomadic groups from the Eurasian Steppes.

The Palace Museum

This museum houses about 700,000 pieces of Chinese artwork and artifacts.

Summer Palace

This palace was once an imperial garden during the Qing dynasty but was destroyed during the Second Opium War of the 1850’s and was rebuilt to be a public park.

Temple of Heaven

This temple symbolizes the relationship between earth and heaven and is located in southern Beijing.


Places to Visit in Shanghai


Yu Garden

This garden was first built during the Ming dynasty and is surrounded by the city god temple and Yuyuan Bazaar.

Shanghai Museum

This museum is considered China’s first world-class modern museum and contains rare cultural pieces.

Oriental Pearl TV Tower

This tower has 15 observational levels with the highest being 1,150 feet above ground.


Places to Visit in Hong Kong


Nan Lian Garden

This Tang dynasty style garden was a joint project between Chi Lin Nunnery and the Hong Kong government built in 2006.

Hong Kong Disneyland

This is the largest theme park in Hong Kong.

Tian Tan Buddha

This bronze statue was built in 1993 and symbolizes the relationship between man and nature.
