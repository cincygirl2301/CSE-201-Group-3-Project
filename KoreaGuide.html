Some Facts about South Korea

South Korea, is a country located in East Asia. It has a population of approximately 52 million people and is known for its rapid economic development, technological advancements, and popular culture. The capital city of Seoul is one of the largest cities in the world. The country is famous for its vibrant K-pop music industry, K-dramas, and movies, which have gained international popularity in recent years. South Korea is also a leader in technology, including the production of smartphones. The country has a rich history and cultural heritage, with traditional arts such as pottery, calligraphy, and martial arts still being widely practiced today. South Korea is also known for its cuisine, including dishes such as kimchi, bibimbap, and Korean BBQ. The country is home to beautiful natural landmarks such as Jeju Island, Seoraksan National Park, and the Han River.

Popular Cities in South Korea


Places to Visit in Seoul


N Seoul Tower

This communication and observation tower is located on Nam Mountain and sits at 774 feet tall. It is South Korea's first general radio wave tower.

Gwangjang Market

This street market is where you can get traditional Korean street food with more than 5,000 shops.

Gyeongbokgung Palace

This palace is the largest of the Five Grand Palaces built by the Joseon dynasty in 1395.


Places to Visit in Busan


Yongdusan Park

This park's most significant structure is the Busan Diamond Tower that has a panoramic view of the park at 393 feet high.

Haeundae Beach

Various festivals are held at this beach throughout the year, including e-sport events and an event that occurs in January where participants bathe in near freezing water.


Places to Visit in Seogwipo


Cheonjiyeon Waterfall

This waterfall is surrounded by a wooded area that is home to a variety of rare plants and large volcanic rocks.

Aqua Planet Jeju

This is the largest public aquarium in South Korea and features over 45,000 marine creatures. Its main tank is 6,000 tons of water filled with about 27,000 fish.
