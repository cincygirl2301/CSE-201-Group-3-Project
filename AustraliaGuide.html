Some Facts About Australia

Australia is the smallest continent while also being the largest country in the world and has a population of approximately 26 million people (as of 2023). 85%-90% of this population lives on the coast due to its milder climate and most of central Australia being a desert. This country has no official language (English is considered their de facto language but it is not stated in their constitution). 80% of the animals found in Australia are unique to this country and you won't find there natural habitat anywhere else in the world.

Places to Visit in Sydney


Sydney Opera House

The Sydney Opera House is an arts center with seven venues located on the Sydney Harbour.

Bondi Beach

Bondi Beach is named after the surrounding suburb and is represented in the National Rugby League competition.

Taronga Zoo Sydney

The Taronga Zoo was the second zoo in Australia to breed a platypus.

Places to Visit in Melbourne


Queen Victoria Market

The Queen Victoria Market is located in the central business district of Melbourne and covers over 17 acres of land.

National Gallery of Victoria

Also known as the NGV, this museum houses art collections in two different locations, NGX International and the Ian Potter Centre: NKV Australia, with a third location to be opened in 2028.
