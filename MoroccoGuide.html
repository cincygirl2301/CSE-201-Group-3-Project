Some Facts about Morocco

Morocco is a country located in North Africa, bordered by the Atlantic Ocean to the west and the Mediterranean Sea to the north. It is the world's largest exporter of phosphates and has a diverse economy that includes agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism. The official languages are Arabic and Berber, and French is also widely spoken. The population is predominantly Muslim, and the country has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its history as a crossroads of different civilizations. Moroccan cuisine is also known for its delicious flavors, with dishes such as tagine and couscous being popular around the world.

Places to Visit in Marrakech


Koutoubia

This building is the largest Mosque in Morocco being 253 feet tall and is considered a classic example of Moroccan Mosque architecture.

House of Photography in Marrakech

This place features about 10,000 documents and vintage photographs from 1870 to 1960, with pictures of Moroccan landscapes, architecural complexes, and portraits.


Places to Visit in Essaouira


Azur Art and Spa

This luxurious spa and wellness center offers a wide range of treatments, including massages, facials, and hammam rituals, in a tranquil serence environment. The center also houses a contemporary art gallery featuring works by local and international artists.

Gallery Kasbah

This gallery includes eleven halls that showcase different artists with works in the style of realism, surrealism, abstract, and impressionism.

Medina of Essaouira

This old 18th century town was an international trading seaport that linked Morocco to Europe.


Places to Visit in Agadir


Agadir Beach

This beach with white sand has a built-in resort set on the mid-Atlantic coast.

Crocoparc

This crocodile park has 325 Nile crocodiles, giant tortoises, green iguanas, anacondas, and marmosets.
