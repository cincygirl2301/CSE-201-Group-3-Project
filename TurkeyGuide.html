Some Facts about Turkey

Turkey is a transcontinental country located at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, with a population of approximately 84 million people. It is known for its diverse culture, rich history, and stunning landscapes. The official language is Turkish, and the country has a predominantly Muslim population. Turkey's economy is primarily based on industries such as tourism, manufacturing, and agriculture, with the country being a major producer of textiles, automotive parts, and food products. The country has a long and storied history, with a number of ancient civilizations leaving their mark, including the Hittites, Greeks, and Romans. Turkey is also home to many historical and cultural landmarks, including the ancient city of Ephesus, the Hagia Sophia in Istanbul, and the fairy chimney rock formations in Cappadocia.

Popular Cities in Turkey


Places to Visit in Istanbul


The Hagia Sophia

This mosque was built during the time of the Byzantine empire and is now a major cultural site in Turkey.

The Blue Mosque

This mosque built in 1616 has blue tiles adorning the interior and is bathed in blue lights.

The Grand Bazaar

This market is one of the biggest of its kind in Istanbul covering 61 streets and contains over 4,00 shops.


Places to Visit in Antalya


Antalya Archeological Museum

This museum is one of the largest in Turkey and is dedicated to the history of the Mediterranean and Pamphylia regions.

Duden Waterfalls

This group of waterfalls occurs as the Duden River splits off the Antalya cliff and into the sea.
