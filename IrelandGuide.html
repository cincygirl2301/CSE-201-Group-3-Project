Some Facts about Ireland

Ireland, also known as the Emerald Isle, is a country located in Western Europe. It is known for its stunning landscapes, rich history, and unique culture. Ireland has a population of approximately 5 million people (as of 2023). Its official languages are English and Irish (Gaelic), with it being known for its folklore, music, and dance. Some of Ireland's famous symbols being Guinness, a harp, and the shamrock which is usually associated with St. Patrick's Day.

Places to Visit in Dublin


Guinness Storehouse

This tourist attraction introduces the ingredients of beer and features the history of Guinness advertising and interactive activities on responsible drinking.

Phoenix Park

This park was once a royal hunting ground due to it being filled with wild fallow deer, which still roam the park to this day.

Dublin Castle

This castle was built in the early 13th century and served as a headquaters for the British administration in Ireland until 1992 when Ireland gained independence.


Places to Visit in Galway


Galway Cathedral

Also known as the Cathedral of Our Lady Assumed into Heaven and St. Nicholas, this cathedral was built in 1965 over the old city prison.

Dunguaire Castle

This castle was built on the southeastern shore of Galway Bay in the 16th century.

Connemara National Park

This park is 2,000 hectares of mountain, bogs, grasslands, and forests. It also features a variety of common song birds and carnivourous plants.
