Some Facts about Country

The Netherlands is a small country located in Western Europe, bordered by Germany to the east, Belgium to the south, and the North Sea to the west and north. The country has a population of approximately 17 million people and is known for its iconic windmills, tulip fields, and historic cities such as Amsterdam, Rotterdam, and the Hague. The official language is Dutch, and the currency is the euro. The Netherlands is a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary system of government, and its economy is known for its strong agricultural, trading, and technological sectors. The country is also known for its progressive social policies, including legalized same-sex marriage.

Popular Cities in The Netherlands


Places to Visit in Amsterdam


Rijksmuseum

This museum is dedicated to Dutch art and history with a collection of about 1 million objects.

Anne Frank House

This biographical is dedicated to Anne Frank and her time hiding from nazi persecution with her family.

Van Gogh Museum

This museum is dedicated to Dutch artist Vincent Van Gogh and contains the largest collection of his works in the world.


Places to Visit in Rotterdam


Museum Boijmans-van Beuningen

This museum has a collection of over 151,000 medieval and contemporary artworks that have been collected over 170 years.

Cube Houses

These houses are based on the idea of "living as an urban roof" according to the architect Piet Blom.

Euromast

This observation tower was built for the 1960 Floriade.


Places to Visit in The Hague


The Pier

This pier contains a range of restaurants and shops, as well as activities like a zipline and ferris wheel.

Peace Palace

This is the international law administrative building that houses the International Court of Justice, the Permanent Court of Arbitration, The Hague Academy of International Law and the Peace Palace Library.
