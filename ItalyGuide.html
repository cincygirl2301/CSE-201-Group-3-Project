Some Facts About Italy

Italy is one of the world’s most important centers of religion, visual arts, literature, music, and culinary arts. Italian farms produce grapes, potatoes, sugar beets, soybeans, grain, and olives. In Italy you can find the Alps which are the world’s most rugged mountains.

Popular Cities in Italy


Places to Visit in Rome


The Colosseum

The Colosseum was built between CE 70 and 72 during the reign of Flavian emperors as a gift to the Roman people that hosted gladiatorial games.

Trevi Fountain

This fountain stands 86 feet tall and 161.3 feet wide and is the largest Baroque fountain in the city.

Sistine Chapel

This mural painted by Michelangelo covers the whole altar wall of the Sistine Chapel.


Places to Visit in Venice


Rialto Bridge

This is the oldest of the four bridges spanning the Grand Canal in Venice.

Gondola Tours

Gondolas are an iconic symbol of Venice, and with so many different routes to take they are good for sightseeing.


Places to Visit in Naples


Pompeii

Pompeii was an ancient city that was buried under volcanic ash from the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD. The city is now available to be toured with an archeologist.
