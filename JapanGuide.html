Some Facts about Japan

Located in Eastern Asia, Japan is an island nation known for its rich history, culture, and technological advancements. It has a population of approximately 126 million people and is known for its highly efficient public transportation system and cutting-edge technology. Japan has a constitutional monarchy with Emperor Naruhito serving as the ceremonial figurehead. This capital city is Tokyo, one of the most populous cities in the world. Japan is renowned for its contributions to fields such as electronics, automotive manufacturing, robotics, and anime. It has a unique and diverse culinary culture, including sushi, ramen, and tempura. Japan is also known for its beautiful natural landscapes, including chery blossoms, Mount Fuji, and hot springs. Japanese arts and traditions such as tea ceremonies, calligraphy, and origami are highly respected and appreciated globally.

Places to Visit in Tokyo


Tokyo Tower

This communication and observation tower was built in 1958 and is 1,092 feet tall. Its inspiration is said to have come from the Eiffel Tower.

Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

This garden was originally the residence of the Naito family in the early 17th century and is home to over 22,000 trees, including cherry blossoms.

Ghibli Museum

This museum shows off work from the animation studio Studio Ghibli and is dedicated to the art and skill of animation.


Places to Visit in Osaka


Osaka Castle

Osaka Castle is a famous Japanese landmark located in the heart of Osaka, known for its grand architecture and historical significance.

Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan

The Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan is one of the largest aquariums in the world, located in Osaka, featuring an enormous tank that houses a variety of marine life, including whale sharks and manta rays.

Kobe Port Tower

This tower is a prominent landmark in the port city of Kobe, known for its unique shape and nighttime illuminations, offering panoramic views of the city and surrounding mountains from its observation deck.
