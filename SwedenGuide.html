Some Facts about Sweden

Sweden is a country located in northern Europe, with a population of approximately 10.3 million people. It is known for its stunning beauty, high standard of living, and progressive social policies. The official language is Swedish, and the country has a rich cultural heritage, including traditional celebrations such as Midsummer and Lucia. Sweden's economy is primarily based on industries such as manufacturing, services, and technology. The country is home to many international companies, including Ikea, Volvo, and Ericsson. Sweden is also known for its welfare programs to all citizens. The country has a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary system of government, and is a member of the European Union. Sweden is also known for its commitment to environmental sustainability, and is a leader in renewable energy and climate action.

Places to Visit in Stockholm


Skansen

This open-air museum and zoo contains a collection of historic structures from Central and Eastern Europe.

The Royal Palace

Take a tour of the official residence of the Swedish royal family.

Vasa Museum

This museum is dedicated to the warship Vasa that is still fully intact after having sank in 1628.


Places to Visit in Gothenburg


Universeum

This science center and museum contains exhibits on space exploration, humans, and technology, and animals such as reptiles and insects.

VOLVO Museum

This museum is dedicated to Sweden's leading vehicular manufacturer Volvo and its development.
