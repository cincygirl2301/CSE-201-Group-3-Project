Some Facts about New Zealand

New Zealand is a small island country located in the southwestern Pacific Ocean. It consists of two main islands, the North Island and the South Island, as well as numerous smaller islands. The country has a population of around 5 million people, and its capital city is Wellington. The official languages are English, Maori, and New Zealand Sign Language. New Zealand is known for its stunning natural landscapes, which include mountains, beaches, and geothermal activity. The country is also renowned for its unique wilflife, such as kiwis, tuataras, and the critically endangered kakapo parrot. The economy is primarily based on tourism, agriculture, and exports of dairyproducts and meat. The country has a strong reputation for environmental protection and social welfare policies.

Places to Visit in Auckland


Sky Tower

This telecommunications and observation tower is 1,076 feet tall and contains restaurants and a cafe.

Auckland Zoo

This zoo plays a part in conservation, research, and education of the species of flor and faunce that live here.

Auckland Art Gallergy Toi o Tamaki

This gallergy has the most extensive collection of national and international art in New Zealand.

Auckland War Memorial Museum

This museum is dedicated to New Zealand's natural and military history.


Places to Visit in Rotorua/h3>

Whakarewarewa Forest

In this forest you can hike, cycle, or ride on horeback through a diverse range of exotic trees.

Polynesian Spa

This spa contains geothermal bathing and access to acidic and alkaline natural mineral springs.


Places to Visit in Christchurch


Christchurch Botanic Gardens

This garden, founded in 1863, is 21 hectacres and has a variety of exotic and local plants, a nursery, several conservatiories, a playground, and a climatological station.

Hagley Park

This is the largest urban park in New Zealand. According to the government's decree at the time of its creation, this park is "reserved forever as a public park, and shall be open for the recreation and enjoyment of the public."

Willowbank Wildlife Reserve

This nature reserve has public displays of various native animal species such as the tuatara and the kiwi.
