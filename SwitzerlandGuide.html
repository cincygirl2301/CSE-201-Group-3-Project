Some Facts about Switzerland

Switzerland is a small landlocked country located in central Europe, with a population of approximately 8.5 million people. It is known for its stunning alpine scenery, high standard of living, and political neutrality. The country has four official languages: German, French, Italian, and Romansh. Switzerland is famous for its banked and financial services, as well as its precision manufacturing industries. The country is also known for its chocolate, cheese, and watches. Switzerland is a federal republic with a direct democratic political system, meaning citizens have a high level of participation in decison-making. The country is home to many international organizations, including the United Nations, the International Committee of the Red Cross, and the World Health Organization. Switzerland is also a popular tourist destination, with attractions such as the Matterhorn, Lake Geneva, and the city of Zurich.

Places to Visit in Zurich


Swiss National Museum

This museum is dedicated to Switzerland's entire history all the way up to the present day.

Grossmunster

This Protestant church was built in 1220 and is one of four major churches in Zurich.

Zurich Zoo

This zoo contains 300 species of animals, with its main attraction being the Asian elephants and Masoala Hall.


Places to Visit in Geneva


St. Pierre Cathedral

This gothic-style church was built in the 12th century and was converted into a Reformed Protestant church from a Roman Catholic cathedral.

Geneva Botanical Garden and Greenhouse

This greenhouse contains over 12,000 species of plants from around the world and contains a historical herbarium with about 6 million botanical specimens.

The Geneva Water Fountain

This water fountain was built in 1886 to release excess pressure from the hydraulic plant and shoot water up to 460 feet into the air.
