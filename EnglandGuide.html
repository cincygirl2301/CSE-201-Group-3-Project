Some Facts About England

England was one of the first countries to undergo an Industrial Revolution and was the world's most industrialized country in the early 19th century. England has a population of over 68 million people and is separated from the rest of Europe by the North Sea and the English Channel. The land in England is mostly flat, with the exception of the mountains in the north that are no higher than 1000 in elevation.

Places to Visit in London


The British Museum

This museum is dedicated to human history, art, and culture and contains about eight million works.

London Eye

This is the largest cantilevered observation wheel in Europe, found on the South Bank of the River Thames. It is 443 feet tall and has a diameter of 394 feet and overlooks the west end of the Jubilee Gardens.

Hyde Park

This park is known for its holding of large rock concerts by artists such as Queen, Pink Floyd, and the Rolling Stones. It is also the largest of the Royal Parks in central London.


Places to Visit in Birmingham


The Birmingham Botanical Garden

This garden features seven thousand different plants from all over the world and a collection of exotic birds.

Library of Birmingham

This library was built to replace the Birmingham Central Library in September 2013 and is considered the largest public library in the United Kingdom.
