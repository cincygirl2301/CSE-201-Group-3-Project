Some Facts about Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, officially known as the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, is an island country located in South Asia. It has a population of approximately 22 million people and is known for its natural beaty, rich culture, and history. The official languages are Sinhala and Tamil, although English is also widely spoken. Sri Lanka is famous for its tea plantations, which produce some of the world's finest tea. The country's economy is primarily based on agriculture, tourism, and manufacturing. Sri Lanka has a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, and various species of birds. The country also has a rich cultural heritage, with many ancient temples and monuments, including the ancient city of Anuradhapura and the Sigiriya Rock Fortress.

Places to Visit in Kandy


Royal Botanical Garden Peradeniya

This garden contains 4,000 species of plants and is most known for its collection of orchids.

Kandy Lake

This lake is located by the Temple of the Tooth and was known to the native people as "Sea of Milk"

World Buddhist Museum

This museum is the world's first international Buddhist museum and is dedicated to the expansion of Buddhism around the world.


Places to Visit in Galle


Maritime Museum

This museum is dedicated to the biological and anthropological aspects of the Southern coastal area.

Galle Lighthouse

This lighthouse is Sri Lanka's oldest light station.
