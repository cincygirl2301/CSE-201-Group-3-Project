Some Facts About Egypt

Egypt is home to the Nile River, the longest river in Africa, whose valley and delta was home to the major civilizations of the ancient Middle East and one of the world’s earliest urban and literate societies. Egypt has a population of over 105 million people and the its official language is Arabic. Egypt’s location in the northeastern corner of Africa made this country a major hub for trading between Africa, Europre and Asia.

Popular Cities in Egypt


Places to Visit in Cairo


The Egyptian Museum

This museum contains the largest collection of pharaonic antiquities with over 120,000 items and is the largest museum in Africa.

Khan el-Khalili

This bazaar (marketplace) was established as the center of trade in the Mamluk era and contains many workshops that produce traditional crafts and souvenirs.

Great Sphinx of Giza

This limestone statue is of a mythical creature with a human head and lion body. It is 240 feet long and 66 feet tall.


Places to Visit in Luxor


Karnak Temple

This complex consists of decayed temples, pylons, and chapels that were built starting as early as 1971-1926 BCE.

Valley of the Kings

This archaeological site was the burial site for all pharaohs from 1539-1075 BCE.

Queen Hatshepsut Temple

This temple was built during the reign of Pharaoh Hatshepsut and is considered a masterpiece of ancient arhitecture.


Places to Visit in Alexandria


Alexandria Library

This was one of the largest libraries of the ancient world. It was a part of a research institute known as the Mouseion, dedicated to the nine goddesses of the arts.

Montaza Palace

This palace was built in 1892 the last Muhammad Ali Dynasty ruler and overlooks the Mediterranean Sea.
