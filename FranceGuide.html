Some Facts About France

France is Europe's most important agricultural producer with the country providing a geographic, economic and linguistic bridge that joins the northern and southern parts of the continent. This country has a population of about 66 million people. French culture has had a large influence on the development of art and science, particularly anthropology, sociology, and philosophy.

Places to Visit in Paris


The Louvre

This is the world's most visited museum and contains artworks and artifacts from 6th century BC to the 19th century, with its best known work being the Mona Lisa.

The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower stands at 1,083 feet tall and was built between the years 1887 and 1889. This tower was built to be the entrance gateway to the International Exposition.

Arc de Triomphe

This national monument took thirty years to build and is considered to be a large part of France’s national identity. Every year the Tour de France bicycle race ends near here.

Notre Dame Cathedral

This cathedral is one of the most famous examples of French gothic architecture. It was finished being built in 1260 and was dedicated to the Virgin Mary.


Places to Visit in Lyon


Museum of Fine Arts of Lyon

This art museum houses art from the time of ancient Egypt all the way to the modern period.

Parc de la Tete d'Or

This 19th century park’s name translates to “Park of the Golden Head.” Boating takes place in the summer and the center of the park is a zoo that can be entered free of charge.

Saint-Jean-Baptiste Cathedral

In December of each year Lyon holds the Festival of Lights where the people will place candles in the windows of this cathedral.
